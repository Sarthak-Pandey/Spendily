Privacy Policy

Last updated: July 9, 2026

1. Introduction

We are Spendly ("we," "us," or "our"). This Privacy Policy explains how we collect, use, disclose, and safeguard your information when you use Spendly, a personal expense-tracking application (the "Service"). Please read this policy together with our Terms and Conditions. By using the Service, you consent to the practices described in this Policy.

2. Data We Collect

We collect the following categories of information: (a) Account data — your email address, display name, and a securely hashed copy of your password; (b) Expense data — the amounts, categories, dates, and descriptions you choose to enter; and (c) Usage data — limited technical information such as device type, browser, and approximate location needed to operate and secure the Service. We do not require you to provide credentials for your bank or other financial accounts.

3. How We Use Your Data

We use your information to: provide, maintain, and improve the Service; authenticate you and secure your account; generate the summaries and category breakdowns you request; communicate with you about your account or changes to the Service; and comply with our legal obligations. We process your data only as necessary to deliver the Service you have requested.

4. Data Storage & Security

Your data is stored on secured servers and is encrypted in transit using HTTPS. Passwords are stored using strong, salted hashing and are never stored in plain text. Access to personal data is restricted to authorised personnel who need it to operate the Service. No method of transmission or storage is completely secure, and while we work to protect your data, we cannot guarantee absolute security. You are responsible for keeping your password confidential.

5. Third-Party Services

We may use third-party service providers for hosting, analytics, and email delivery. These providers may process data on our behalf under appropriate agreements. We do not sell your personal or financial data to third parties. Any third-party services are governed by their own privacy policies, for which we are not responsible.

6. Cookies

We use essential cookies to keep you signed in and to maintain your session as you move through the Service. We do not currently use advertising or cross-site tracking cookies. You can disable cookies in your browser settings, but some features of the Service may not function correctly if you do.

7. Your Rights

You have the right to access, correct, or delete the personal data we hold about you, to export your expense data, and to withdraw consent where processing is based on consent. To exercise these rights, email privacy@spendly.example. We will respond within a reasonable period and may retain certain information as required by law.

8. Changes to This Policy

We may update this Privacy Policy from time to time. When we make changes, we will revise the "Last updated" date above and, where appropriate, notify you through the Service. Your continued use of the Service after changes take effect constitutes your acceptance of the updated Policy.

9. Contact Us

If you have any questions about this Privacy Policy or how we handle your data, please contact us at privacy@spendly.example. For general support, email support@spendly.example.
